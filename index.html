Info

The axolotl (Ambystoma mexicanum) is a crtitically endangered species of salamander endemic to Lake Xochimilco in present-day Mexico City. The name 'axolotl' derives from the dog-like Aztec god Xolotl and the Nahatul word 'atl', meaning water. The species remains aquatic throughout its lifespan, but human inteference, such as the draining of the lakes surrounding Mexico City in 16th century, as well as water pollution, introduction of invasive aquatic species, and more has lead to the decline in the axolotl population. Axolotls have the ability to regenerate limbs and even parts of the brain. While wild populations struggle, axolotls are sometimes held in captivity as exotic pets for aquariums.

Colors

While wild axolotls are typically a dark gray or black in order to blend in with the water of their habitat, captive axolotls can be specifically bred to display a variety of colors. Leucism can occur in many animals, including axolotls, and it describes the loss of pigmentation, causing the animal to appear pale or white. In axolotls, this causes them to appear a bright pink with dark eyes. The similar condition called albinism also results in a pale pink body, but with reddish eyes. Piebald axolotls display both the dark grays of wild axolotls mixed with the pink of leucistic axolotls, creating a spotted appearence of two colors.
